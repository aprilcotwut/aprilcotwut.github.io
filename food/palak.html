Palak Paneer / Chole

ingredients

  • ghee (or oil AND butter)
  • splash of vinegar
  • 350 g spinach, w stems (2 bunches at ONF)
  • 3 cluster tomatoes (or, like 4 roma?)
  • 1 onion
  • 10ish garlic cloves
  • 1-3 serrano, or sub cayenne powder
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger
  • 2 cardamom pods
  • ~1/2 tbsp cumin seeds
  • cayenne powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala (Penzey's)
  • 1.5 tsp hot curry powder (Penzey's)
  • 200g chickpeas OR 1 package of paneer

notes

i literally do not know what im doing here as i have no connection to this culture so if you have tips... ping me

directions

if you are making chole, soak day before and then boil (or boil a can).

begin cleaning spinach so you can continue while veggies + spices cook.

use a stainless steel pan!!

heat ghee (or oil), cut up your onion into 4ths, smoosh cardamom, and fry all on medium. it should be a bit crispy, and stick to pan.

add other spices except cayenne powder and some of hot curry powder for a minute or two. while those are cooking, begin boiling water to blanch spinach.

cut tomatoes into 4ths and add to pan. this should help deglaze.

blanch spinach w/ splash of vinegar

blend your toasted veggies, w/ a ice cube if you're worried about explosion. set aside

lay out your spinach length wise. w/ some of your blended veggies, blend spinach stems (and some leaf). cut leaves into 1 cm chunks.

combine all in original pan, don't clean any remaining spices/ crisp. cook on low/medium.

fry your paneer or your chole in some ghee (or butter) w/ remaining hot curry powder. for paneer, make sure it gets a golden edge on at least a few sides.

combine goop w your protein

enjoy!!
